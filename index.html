What is it?

WeatherBlend fetches forecasts from multiple global weather models simultaneously and blends them. Research shows multi-model blends are consistently more accurate than any single model.

The cards view

The big now card shows current conditions for the selected day: a data-driven blend orb, temperature (with feels-like and the day's high/low), rain, wind and cloud. Swipe left/right between days or tap ◀ ▶. Below it, four sparklines (temp, rain, wind, cloud) trace the whole day — solid where observed, dashed where forecast, with sunrise/sunset markers and a red "now" line. On today you can drag the now line to scrub the card to any hour.

The metric cards and hourly strip always reconcile with the table — the same blended (or observed) value appears everywhere.

Reading the table

Each section heading row is the blended average. Open ☰ → Models & sources and enable Sources to see individual model rows below it, each tagged with its current accuracy weight. The ✓ Actual row shows real observations for past hours (from your chosen source). A Confidence row in each section shows the per-hour model agreement for that metric. Daytime columns are shaded lighter; a red column marks the current hour.

Accuracy, weighting & bias

Models are blended by how accurate they've recently been, scored against real observations. Each model's error is tracked per metric and per lead time; skill is sharpened (inverse-squared error), shrunk toward equal until enough evidence accumulates, and clamped so no model is silenced or dominates. Each model's systematic bias (e.g. always forecasting 0.8° too cold here) is also learned and subtracted before blending — for temperature, wind and cloud. Rain is scored on occurrence (did it rain when the model said so?) plus error on wet days, so a model can't look good by always predicting dry.

Actual source sets the ground truth: BOM (station obs), Open-Meteo (model mean) or Blend. Weight method: Recency — recency-weighted error (plus server-learned weights and biases when BOM is the truth source). Per-day — scores each model per day over the last X days. Blend — the mean of the two.

Confidence

Confidence is a plain-English read on how much the models agree. When they all predict much the same thing it's High; when they diverge it falls to Medium or Low. It's computed per metric, per hour and per day. It measures agreement, not certainty.

The forecast glyph

Instead of fixed weather icons, the now card draws one living mark built from the data itself: four circles — mint (temperature), lime (wind), blue (rain) and purple (cloud) — each growing and brightening with its value, with the strongest metric glowing. Every forecast gets its own visual fingerprint.

Location

Tap the location name to search any city (GPS first, saved fallback). Viewing a place in another timezone opens the cards and hourly strip on that location's current time.
